# Content Writer

Write professional content for announcements and brand communications.

## When to Use

- Writing blog posts or articles for a crypto project
- Creating tweet threads for social accounts
- Drafting announcements (product launches, partnerships, upgrades)
- Educational content explaining mechanisms or features
- Any content representing a crypto brand

## What to Ask For Upfront

If not provided, ask for:
1. **Project name and what it does** (one sentence)
2. **Brand voice** (calm/technical? energetic? institutional?)
3. **Audience** (traders, developers, DeFi users, general crypto?)
4. **Content type** (announcement, explainer, thread, full article)

## Default Brand Voice

Calm, confident, technically precise. Never hype-y or desperate. Lead with the problem, then the solution. Explain technical concepts simply without dumbing down. Respect the reader's intelligence.

Adapt tone to the brand when provided — but always stay human.

## Critical Style Rules

**Write like a human, not like AI:**
- No short, choppy sentences that feel robotic
- Combine related ideas into flowing sentences that move naturally
- Never use em dashes (AI tell) — use commas, parentheses, or restructure
- Never use horizontal dividers (---) between sections
- No hype words: "exciting," "thrilled," "game-changing," "revolutionary"
- No emojis in articles (tweet threads can have one or two)

## Article Structure

Open with a **bold TL;DR** (one or two sentences capturing the core insight). Then move through:
1. **Problem** — why the reader should care
2. **Solution** — what's changing and how it works
3. **Outcome** — benefits and timeline

Don't pad sections to look substantial. A two-sentence section is fine if that's all it takes.

## Tweet Threads

Maximum **3 tweets** unless specifically requested longer:
1. **Hook** — the problem and why it matters
2. **Solution** — mechanism + what makes it different
3. **Outcome** — timeline + impact + optional link

No hashtags. Tag partners with @. Short enough to scan, not so choppy they feel robotic.

## Output Format

Always deliver both:
1. **Article** — formatted with headers, ready to paste into any CMS or Notion
2. **Tweet thread** — numbered, copy-paste ready

## Quality Checklist

Before delivering: problem stated clearly, technical concepts explained, no hype words, no em dashes, no horizontal dividers, active voice, flowing sentences, tweet thread 3 or fewer, concrete timeline mentioned, partner tags correct.
