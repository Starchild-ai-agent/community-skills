# Crypto Content Patterns

## Article Structure

Keep articles brief. People don't have long attention spans, so every paragraph needs to earn its place. If a section doesn't add value, cut it.

**Never use horizontal dividers (---) between sections.** They signal AI-generated content and break reading flow. Let headers do the work.

### General Flow

Open with a bold TL;DR that captures the core insight in one or two sentences. Then move through the problem (why should the reader care), the solution (what's changing and how it works), and close with what this means for them (benefits and timeline).

Don't pad sections to make them look substantial. A two-sentence section is fine if that's all it takes to make the point.

### Product Launch

Title should include the product name and a one-line value prop. Open with a TL;DR, then cover the problem it solves, how the product works (use numbered steps if the mechanism has a clear sequence), key benefits, and what's next with a specific timeline.

### Partnership Announcement

Title format: "[Project] + [Partner]: [What this enables]". Cover why this partner specifically, what it means for users in concrete terms, how the integration works if relevant, and when users can expect it.

### Technical Upgrade

Title should name the upgrade and the problem it solves. TL;DR should capture the before and after in one sentence. Cover the problem, the solution, optional technical details for users who want depth, and the expected impact with numbers if you have them.

### Educational Explainer

Title should be a question or a clear statement of the concept. Open by establishing why this matters to the reader right now. Explain the current situation (what problem exists or what confusion is common), then the concept itself, then how it applies to the project or the reader's situation. Close with a concrete takeaway.

## Tweet Threads

**Maximum three tweets** unless specifically asked for more.

First tweet hooks with the problem and why it matters to the reader. Second tweet introduces the solution, explains the mechanism briefly, and highlights what makes it different. Third tweet states the outcome, gives a timeline, and optionally links to the full article.

Don't use hashtags. Tag partners with @. Keep sentences short enough to scan quickly but not so choppy they feel robotic.

## Quality Checklist

**Before delivering an article:** title is specific, TL;DR captures the core insight, problem section creates stakes, solution is clear, benefits are concrete, timeline is specific, no hype words, no em dashes, no horizontal dividers, proofread for typos.

**Before delivering tweets:** hook in first tweet, max three tweets unless asked, partner tags correct, no hashtags, clear outcome, line breaks for readability.
